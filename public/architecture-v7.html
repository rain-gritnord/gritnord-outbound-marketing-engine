Why broad content fails here
250 UC candidates/month is a small, high-value pool. SEO and LinkedIn posts reach thousands of irrelevant people and maybe 2–3 of the right ones. Content ROI is near zero at this scale. The right 250 won't self-select fast enough to hit 10 UCs/month.
Why hyper-personal works
A video that says your name + references your last LinkedIn post + shows your pain in a live dashboard is impossible to ignore. It's not a pitch — it's proof. The UC sees Gritnord doing for itself exactly what they'll be paying for. Conversion rate is 4–6× higher than generic outbound.
The operational reality
Clay automates the research. Claude writes the angle. HeyGen renders 250 personalised videos in ~2h. Rain records 5 voice note templates (by vertical) — Make.com splices the name. Each "personal" touch costs <€2 to produce. Total batch cost: <€500 to reach 250 perfectly-targeted UCs.

DC → UC trust flow — why DCs show up to meetings
🎯
Problem 1: DC doesn't know who's calling
Cold outreach = ignored without brand recognition.
Fix: paid social + cookie retargeting starts D1. DC sees Gritnord/UC brand 3–5 times before first DM arrives. Warm before touched.
🤔
Problem 2: DC doesn't trust the offer
Generic pitch = no credibility. DC has seen 50 like it.
Fix: HeyGen video says DC's name + company. UC founder voice note is personal. LinkedIn DM references specific DC pain (Claude-researched). Not generic — provably personalised.
⏱️
Problem 3: DC doesn't have time
Even interested DCs don't act unless friction is near zero.
Fix: one-tap Calendly in every message. Ask for 15 min max. WhatsApp message is 2 sentences. BDR call scripts are 90 sec qualification only. Lowest possible ask.
💡
Problem 4: DC forgets between touches
Sequence gaps = DC goes cold. Each touch feels disconnected.
Fix: every touch references the previous one. "I sent you a video Tuesday…" — continuous narrative. DC feels followed, not spammed. Cookie retargeting keeps brand visible between all touches.
★ The core insight: DCs don't show up to meetings because of one great message. They show up because they've seen the brand 7+ times across different channels and the ask is frictionless. Volume × personalisation × ease = meetings booked.
