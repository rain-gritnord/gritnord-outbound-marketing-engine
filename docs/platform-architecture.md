# Gritnord Full Platform Architecture
**Current Version — updated with every flow change**
Last updated: May 2026

---

## PHASE 0 — Pre-Sales Gate

1. UC discovers Gritnord via content marketing, LinkedIn, referral, or DC→UC loop
2. UC lands on `gritnord.com` — value prop, pricing visible, case stats, brands served
3. UC books 30-min discovery call — Gritnord qualifies: right size, right problem, right market
4. UC accepts T&Cs → taken directly to Stripe payment page (no link sent, direct redirect)

---

## PHASE 0.5 — Free Trial / Lead Magnet

5. UC registers on `app.gritnord.com` free — Google OAuth or password
6. Platform auto-generates from company URL + LinkedIn + short intake form:
   - Draft messaging and pitch in UC's voice
   - 2–3 sample DC personas with outreach angle
   - 1 lead magnet concept for their industry
7. UC sees what Gritnord delivers before committing — this is the conversion moment
8. UC converts to paid → Stripe payment page for €3,500 onboarding
9. Billing details captured before payment: company name, registration number, VAT, invoicing address, credit card on file
10. Any docs uploaded during trial carry over automatically to paid onboarding

---

## PHASE 1 — Onboarding Preparation

11. UC books onboarding meeting from Gritnord calendar — 3 hours (2026 standard), 90 minutes (future target)
12. UC uploads docs — strategy, pitch, value prop, pricing, competitor analysis, ICP research, case studies. Trial uploads shown first, UC confirms or adds more
13. Platform prompts UC before meeting: "Review your top 3 existing customers — what made them convert? These become your ICP foundation."
14. Gritnord team reads all docs before the meeting — no discovery during the session
15. UC confirms DC meeting team details on app: name, LinkedIn, mobile, email, calendar booking link per person
16. Comms channel set up same day: WhatsApp, Slack, or Telegram

---

## PHASE 2 — Onboarding Meeting

17. 3-hour session — face-to-face preferred, online when travel is difficult
18. Meeting recorded — audio + transcript auto-generated
19. UC sees: recording + full transcript
20. Gritnord writes internal memo — not visible to UC
21. During meeting: UC names top 3 existing customers, explains what made them convert — this anchors ICP and persona modelling
22. One sample ICP + one sample persona built live during the meeting — UC understands the logic
23. End of meeting = end of onboarding session → Lock and Load checklist appears

---

## PHASE 3 — AI Processing (target: 48–72 hours)

24. AI ingests all uploaded docs + transcript + Gritnord memo + trial outputs simultaneously
25. AI generates full ICP definition: industry, company size, geography, tech stack, buying triggers, deal cycle
26. First batch: 100 DC contacts (not 250 — validate ICP fit via cold calling before scaling)
27. Minimum 2 personas per ICP: title, pain, buying role, common objections, preferred channel, language patterns
28. Per each ICP + persona, location-based: top 3 industry events they attend + top 2–3 channels where they get information (trade publications, LinkedIn groups, newsletters, podcasts)
29. 3–5 lead magnet concepts ranked by expected DC conversion — includes competitor research and what DCs in this vertical typically respond to
30. Pitch scenario and messaging per persona — written in UC's voice
31. Content marketing 90-day calendar: per post — topic, persona, channel, goal, CTA — written so AI executes; Gritnord team can execute manually when needed
32. Markets to target confirmed — defined during onboarding, locked here
33. Gritnord team reviews and approves all output — UC does not approve the list

---

## PHASE 4 — Infrastructure Setup (parallel to Phase 3)

34. New domain purchased per UC (e.g., `goglobalreader.com`) — registered in Lemlist or Cloudflare
35. Domain email addresses created per Gritnord team member assigned to this UC
36. Email warmup starts immediately — secondary channel; primary focus is content marketing, SEO, AI search, and direct persona-channel outreach (LinkedIn, Facebook, WhatsApp, industry platforms)
37. SEO + AI search compatibility audit run on UC's website and LinkedIn:
    - Technical SEO issues flagged
    - AI search visibility checked: Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini, Google AI Overviews
    - Structured data gaps identified
    - Immediate fixes prioritised: fix now / fix this month / fix later
    - Gritnord resells a 3rd-party SEO/AI-search platform (Semrush, BrightEdge, or Search Atlas)
38. DC landing page built — visual replica of UC's homepage, Gritnord-hosted:
    - Cookie consent banner — legitimate interest, GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)
    - UTM tracking on all DC inbound
    - Soft CTA: "Book a meeting with [UC company name]"
    - Cookie drop on every DC visit
    - Any DC who books via this page = Gritnord attributed, billed at €500
39. Lead magnets built as interactive web pages — not PDFs, keeps DC engaged on site
40. 100 DC contacts uploaded to HubSpot → segmented by ICP + persona → assigned to Gritnord team members for cold calling

## PHASE 5 — Lock and Load

41. Gritnord team completes 10-item checklist before activation:
    - DC team member calendar links confirmed
    - Comms channel live
    - 100 DC contacts validated in HubSpot and assigned to team members
    - ICP and personas approved internally
    - Lead magnets live as interactive pages
    - DC landing page live with cookie tracking
    - SEO audit delivered to UC
    - 90-day content calendar scheduled
    - Billing card confirmed
    - Domain and emails ready
42. Gritnord clicks: **Start Outreach Campaign**
43. What fires simultaneously:
    - Cold calling begins (100 DC first batch — validate ICP before scaling to 250/month)
    - LinkedIn direct outreach per persona
    - WhatsApp outreach where applicable
    - Facebook outreach where applicable
    - Industry channel outreach: publications, groups, events mapped per ICP
    - Content marketing publishing begins
    - Targeted ads deploy (if UC subscribed to traffic package)
    - AI tracking campaigns live
    - SEO implementation begins on UC site based on audit

## PHASE 6 — Meeting Operations

44. Meeting booked → automatic sequence fires immediately:
    - Confirmation to both DC and UC team member
    - 24 hours before: reminder with context — who they're meeting, why, what to prepare
    - 15 minutes before: check-in ping via WhatsApp + email
    - Meeting start time: if either party hasn't joined within 5 minutes → WhatsApp + email + automated call to missing party
    - If can't attend: immediate rebook — 3 time options sent automatically
45. Meeting held → €500 charged immediately to UC credit card
46. No-show = no charge. Reschedule logged and tracked.
47. No-show rate monitored per UC — if rising, flagged for human review

## PHASE 7 — DC Loyal Network

48. Every DC who attends a Gritnord-facilitated meeting enters the network
49. DC network segmented by: industry, company size, role, deal appetite, geography
50. When new UC signs up whose ICP matches an existing network DC: warm outreach sent — "We have a company we think you should meet, based on your business"
51. Response rate on warm DC outreach: 5–10× cold outreach — trust already established
52. Over time, loyal DCs accept meetings based on a WhatsApp message alone — zero friction
53. As DC network grows: cost per meeting drops, margins expand automatically
54. This is the structural moat — competitors without the network cannot replicate it

## CROSS-CUTTING — Self-Learning Layer

Runs underneath all phases continuously.

63. **Layer 1 — Campaign Feedback Loop:** every outreach action logged (opened / replied / clicked / booked / ignored). After 50 data points per variable, system adjusts: subject lines, send times, CTAs, channel mix per ICP. No human needed.
64. **Layer 2 — Cross-UC Learning (anonymised):** what works for one SaaS UC targeting VP Sales in Nordics becomes the starting point for the next similar UC. Each new UC starts with the best known playbook, not a blank slate.
65. **Layer 3 — Content Performance:** every post, lead magnet, blog article tracked for engagement + DC landing page referrals + meetings converted. Content calendar rewrites itself based on what generates pipeline.
66. **Layer 4 — Industry Intelligence (daily):** monitors B2B sales methodology, AI sales tools, competitor moves, content marketing, SEO/AI search changes, regulatory updates. High-impact items auto-routed to relevant modules. Rain reviews weekly brief — approves or overrides.
67. **Layer 5 — Meeting Quality Feedback:** Gritnord team logs post-meeting: right DC? persona held? pitch angle correct? DC advancing toward deal? Feeds back into ICP refinement. After 10 meetings per UC, system predicts which DCs will advance vs ghost.
68. **Layer 6 — UC Health Monitoring:** tracks per UC: meetings held, UC team attendance, DC pipeline advancing, no-show rate, platform engagement. Early warning flags 6 weeks before potential churn — human review triggered.

## CONTENT ENGINE — Build Principles

69. One clean Claude call per article — no retry loops, no second trim call
70. 7-mechanism POST_PROMPT is the core — do not change the structure
71. CANNOT_GENERATE is a soft skip signal — article lacks confrontable numbers, skip and move on
72. Rule 1 and character count logged as warnings — Rain reviews in approval queue, no auto-block
73. Curator requires 2+ numbers in article title+description before sending to Claude — better input = better output
74. Post performance analyser: scan scored posts, find patterns from top performers, encode as evidence in prompt
75. Source diversity not enforced — if one source consistently produces data-rich articles, use it multiple times
